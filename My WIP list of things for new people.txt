The game of GGN is confusing at first. So, here are some tips.

Pets
- Not sure what pets to get first? Quartz Dwarf and a Mario/Whale pet are good when you have no idea what you want. Just hold them while you figure things out and you won't regret it. Need one? PM me, I have tons.
- Why Quartz/Mario/Whale? Because they drop items that you can both use and trade with, for a good length of time before being useless. Great as a starter set until you learn the game and know what you want.
- Basically, a "pet" is an item you equip that works 24/7 around the clock and generates items. Different pets "drop" different items. They earn 1 XP per hour, and level up to have better odds/chance of dropping the item. It's best to get a pet as soon as you can since you can't get time back!

Cards
- "Someone told me to never open my random cards because they're so valuable!!!!" -- not great advice. Here's what you need to understand: Opening cards allows you to build a collection. You combine specific cards together to make "stronger" cards, and eventually a lootbox which you can open for prizes. Now, if you never open your cards, you'll never be able to get this. So why would someone tell you a "random" card is more valuable? Simple. If I have 100 luigi cards, I don't need another one. So if you try to trade me one, I'll say no. BUT, if you have a "Random Card" then I'll trade for it, because maybe it's NOT a luigi card. Make sense? Random cards are easier to trade with because of that--therefore, they're move "valuable" in a trade than an opened card. It's as simple as that.

Equipment Tips
- Golden gloves will double your forum posts and IRC lines -- pretty handy.
- Empowered Quartz of Fortune breaks even at 10gph. Jade breaks even at 33gph.
- In 95% of cases, empowered jewelry is the better move compared to non-empowered jewelry.
- Ruby Baguettes break even at ~277gph. Using it before that is a waste and it's better to use it as a trade item.

Items in the Shop
- Why is everything "out of stock" ? -- This is because those items cannot be purchased, they can only be traded for (or crafted).

Leveling
- You level up your class by hitting achievements -- I have a fast level-up guide down below.

Trading
- Want trading to be less confusing? Don't trade until you have a goal in mind -- this simplifies everything. Are you wanting a dragon? Are you wanting to idle and get tons of gold? Are you wanting to collect random pets? Are you wanting to just cover your upload/download usage? Figure out a goal first.

- Trading is all about supply/demand. Not just the supply/demand of the market, but also your own personal supply/demand. Don't get bamboozled into thinking your item is worth billions -- to the right person it might be, but to the person you're trading with? Maybe not. Just know what you want, and know what you're fine giving up. Sometimes it's worth losing a little on a trade now to set yourself up for an advantage later.

- "Filler" is a word for junk. Basically, common items that most people don't use much. When you make a trade, it has to be a 60%/40% split at minimum to be valid. So if you're trading a 3000 gold item for a 100,000 gold item, there is a huge discrepancy in the trade value (greater than 60%/40%), so you need to add more items (things you don't want, aka "filler") to the trade to get into the 60/40 range so the trade can happen. MagusV has a handy filler guide on his profile -- but it's not the end-all-be-all, just a helpful guide. Anything can be filler if you don't want it.
